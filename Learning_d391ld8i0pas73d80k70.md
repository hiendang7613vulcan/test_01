## ðŸ“ Summary

This lesson explores how personal values, beliefs, and behaviour shape professional practice, especially in clinical or workplace settings. Values are the core principles that guide what we find important, like honesty or punctuality. Beliefs are the things we accept as true, whether based on evidence, probability, or faith. Behaviour is how we act, particularly around others. Together, these elements influence how we interact with colleagues and clients. While positive values can enhance professional performance, problems arise when we impose our values or express divisive beliefs in the workplace. Such actions can lead to conflict, alienation, and even loss of clients or reputation. Professionals must be self-aware, respect others' differences, and maintain boundaries to ensure a healthy, effective work environment.

### ðŸ“Œ Key Points

- Personal values, beliefs, and behaviour impact professional practice.
- Positive values like honesty and commitment benefit the workplace.
- Imposing your values or beliefs on others can cause conflict.
- Avoid expressing strong political, religious, or spiritual beliefs at work.
- Negative effects of unprofessional behaviour include loss of clients and damaged reputation.

## ðŸ“– Concepts & Definitions

### ðŸ”¹ Values

**Definition:** Values are what an individual holds to be important in their life, guiding how they order their lives and make decisions.

**Analogy:** It's like a compass that points you in the direction you want to go in life.

**Examples:** Valuing honesty and always telling the truth.; Valuing punctuality and always being on time.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Confusing values with temporary preferences.


### ðŸ”¹ Beliefs

**Definition:** A belief is an acceptance that something is true, which can be based on certainty, probability, or faith.

**Analogy:** It's like wearing a pair of glasses that color how you see the world.

**Examples:** Believing that hard work leads to success.; Believing in a particular scientific principle.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Assuming everyone shares your beliefs.


### ðŸ”¹ Behaviour

**Definition:** Behaviour is the way an individual conducts themselves, especially around others.

**Analogy:** It's like the actions you take on a stage that others can see.

**Examples:** Greeting colleagues politely every morning.; Listening attentively during meetings.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Thinking behaviour is only about big actions, not everyday habits.


### ðŸ”¹ Imposing Values and Beliefs

**Definition:** Forcing or pressuring others to accept or follow your own values and beliefs.

**Analogy:** It's like trying to make everyone wear the same shoes, even if they don't fit.

**Examples:** Telling a coworker they should share your religious views.; Judging a colleague for having different priorities.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Sharing opinions respectfully without expecting agreement.
