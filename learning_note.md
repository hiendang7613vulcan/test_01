## ğŸ“ Summary

Ho Chi Minh City is located in southern Vietnam and is the most populous and economically important city in the country. Formerly known as Saigon, this name is still widely used today. The city was called the 'Paris of the East' during the French colonial period, characterized by French-influenced architecture and streetscapes. Ho Chi Minh City accounts for about half of Vietnam's GDP and was historically known by the Khmer name Prey Nokor, meaning 'forest city.' The city's development took shape through French colonial rule and the Vietnam War, making it a significant symbol of Vietnam's culture, economy, and history.
