## ðŸ“– Concepts & Definitions

### ðŸ”¹ Bias

**Definition:** A tendency to favor or lean towards a particular perspective, often unconsciously, which can affect judgment and behavior.

**Analogy:** It's like wearing tinted glasses that subtly change how you see everything, even if you don't notice.

**Examples:** Preferring to listen to people who share your background.; Assuming a client will react a certain way based on stereotypes.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Being completely objective at all times (which is unrealistic).


### ðŸ”¹ Diversity

**Definition:** The presence of differences within a group, including backgrounds, lifestyles, and perspectives.

**Analogy:** It's like a garden with many types of flowers, each adding to the overall beauty and richness.

**Examples:** Working with clients from various cultures.; Recognizing different family structures.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Assuming everyone thinks or feels the same way.


### ðŸ”¹ Neutral Environment (in therapy)

**Definition:** A setting where the therapist does not impose personal opinions or preferences, allowing the client to express themselves freely.

**Analogy:** It's like a blank canvas where the client can paint their own picture without interference.

**Examples:** Not sharing your own beliefs during a session.; Letting the client lead the discussion.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Giving advice based on your own values.; Steering the conversation to topics you prefer.
