## ðŸ“– Concepts & Definitions

### ðŸ”¹ Bias

**Definition:** A tendency to favor or be against something, often in a way that is unfair or not based on objective reasoning.

**Analogy:** It's like wearing tinted glasses that color everything you see, making it hard to see things as they really are.

**Examples:** Assuming someone is less capable because of their background.; Preferring people who share your interests.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Believing you have no biases at all.; Assuming bias only applies to negative attitudes.


### ðŸ”¹ Self-awareness

**Definition:** The ability to recognize and understand your own thoughts, feelings, and biases.

**Analogy:** It's like looking in a mirror to see not just your face, but your thoughts and motivations.

**Examples:** Noticing you interrupt certain people more than others.; Realizing you feel uncomfortable around unfamiliar cultures.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Ignoring your own reactions.; Assuming self-awareness is automatic.


### ðŸ”¹ Neutral environment (in therapy)

**Definition:** A setting where the therapist does not influence the client with personal opinions, allowing the client to express themselves freely.

**Analogy:** It's like a blank canvas where the client can paint their own picture without interference.

**Examples:** Not sharing your political views with a client.; Avoiding judgmental language.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Telling clients what you would do in their situation.; Letting your preferences shape the session.


### ðŸ”¹ Embracing diversity

**Definition:** Accepting and valuing differences among people, including backgrounds, lifestyles, and perspectives.

**Analogy:** It's like adding different spices to a dish to make it richer and more flavorful.

**Examples:** Listening to clients from various cultures with an open mind.; Appreciating different family structures.

**Common Pitfalls / Non-examples:** Pretending differences don't exist.; Assuming everyone should think or act the same.
